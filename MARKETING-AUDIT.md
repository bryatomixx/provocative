## Executive Summary

Provocative Plastic Surgery is a Miami-based plastic and aesthetic surgery practice operating with two board-certified surgeons out of a premium location five minutes from Miami International Airport. With an overall marketing score of **50/100**, the practice sits in a position that is both frustrating and encouraging: the raw inputs are genuinely strong, but the marketing infrastructure built on top of them is failing to convert those assets into patient volume and revenue.

The biggest strength is clear: Dr. Yoel A. Rojas Ortiz carries a credential stack — ABPS board certification, three consecutive Newsweek Best Plastic Surgeons designations (2021/22/23), and NYT Super Doctors recognition — that most Miami competitors simply cannot match. The brand name "Provocative" is one of the few truly differentiated identities in a market drowning in "Premier," "Elite," and "Prestige" practices. The geographic location adjacent to MIA positions the practice for medical tourism revenue that most local competitors lack the logistics to pursue.

The biggest gap is equally clear: the practice is effectively invisible where patients make decisions. There is virtually no review presence on Google, Yelp, or RealSelf. The website runs on a Wix platform with no structured data, orphaned draft pages contaminating the sitemap, and zero pricing transparency — all of which cause high-intent prospects to leave before ever making contact. The conversion funnel leaks badly.

The top three actions that would move the needle most are: **(1)** an emergency review acquisition campaign targeting 50+ Google reviews and a live RealSelf profile within 90 days, **(2)** replacing the generic "Miami's premier destination" positioning with copy that surfaces Dr. Rojas's Newsweek credentials on every patient touchpoint, and **(3)** cleaning the Wix sitemap of orphaned pages and injecting LocalBusiness + Physician schema markup. Combined, these three interventions could realistically deliver **$15,000–$35,000/month in additional consultation revenue** within 6 months by closing the most visible trust and discoverability gaps.

Implementing the full set of recommendations across all six dimensions would put the practice in a 70+ scoring range within 12 months and conservatively add **$40,000–$80,000/month** in attributable revenue from improved organic visibility, higher consultation-to-booking rates, and activation of the medical tourism channel.

## Quick Wins (This Week)

**1. Remove orphaned pages from the Wix sitemap**
Go to Wix Editor → open each of the four junk pages (`blank`, `blank-1`, `blank-2`, `home-2-1-1-1`) → Settings → SEO → toggle "Hide page from search results." Then submit the refreshed `sitemap.xml` to Google Search Console.
*Why it matters:* 8.3% of the indexed sitemap is draft debris. Googlebot wastes crawl cycles on shell pages instead of procedure pages.
*Estimated impact:* Unlocks crawl budget for revenue-generating pages; eliminates thin-content quality signal.

**2. Update the Google Business Profile to lead with Newsweek recognition**
First sentence: *"Provocative Plastic Surgery is home to Dr. Yoel A. Rojas — named one of Newsweek's Best Plastic Surgeons three consecutive years (2021, 2022, 2023). Board-certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery. Located 5 minutes from Miami International Airport."* Add all procedure categories, confirm address/hours/phone, upload 20+ additional photos.
*Why it matters:* The GBP card is the first trust signal Google shows in local searches. Most patients decide whether to click in under 5 seconds based on this card.
*Estimated impact:* 10–15% lift in click-through from local search results.

**3. Replace the homepage tagline with POV copy**
Kill "Miami's premier destination for plastic surgery & med-spa treatments." Every competitor says some version of this. Proposed replacement: *"Bold results for patients who don't do subtle."* or *"Surgery confident enough to match the city."* The tagline must sound like it belongs to a practice called Provocative — not Premier or Elite.
*Why it matters:* The brand name earns attention; a generic tagline immediately squanders it.
*Estimated impact:* Reduced bounce rate; stronger brand recall; sets up all downstream conversion improvements.

**4. Audit and fix the robots.txt lightbox disallow rule**
The current `robots.txt` blocks `*?lightbox=` for ALL bots, meaning gallery image URLs are completely uncrawlable by Googlebot. Modify to disallow only low-value scrapers, or use `noindex` meta tags on lightbox variants instead. This opens gallery images to Google Images search.
*Why it matters:* Before/after gallery image search is high-intent and currently 100% blocked.
*Estimated impact:* New inbound discovery channel with zero additional content investment.

**5. Add "Starting at $X" price anchors to every major procedure page**
On each procedure page (BBL, breast augmentation, tummy tuck, rhinoplasty, liposuction, mommy makeover), add one line adjacent to the primary CTA: *"Procedures typically start at $[X] — financing available from $[Y]/month through PatientFi and Cherry."* Link both financing pages. Do not publish a full price list — just a directional anchor.
*Why it matters:* Price is the most Googled secondary query after any plastic surgery procedure name. Patients who cannot find a range leave — and most will not call to ask.
*Estimated impact:* 15–25% lift in consultation request rate on procedure pages.

**6. Launch an automated post-visit Google review request sequence**
SMS at 10–14 days post-procedure with a direct deep-link to the Google review form; email follow-up at 6 weeks. Most plastic surgery patients are highly satisfied and willing to review — they simply need to be asked at the right moment with zero friction.
*Why it matters:* The practice currently has critically thin review presence. Competitors with 80+ reviews consistently outrank in local search.
*Estimated impact:* 50+ reviews within 90 days; meaningful local SEO rank lift within 6 months.

## Strategic Recommendations (This Month)

**1. Inject LocalBusiness + Physician schema markup via Wix Custom Code**
Use Wix's Settings → Custom Code → Head to inject JSON-LD structured data covering `MedicalClinic`, `Physician` (both Dr. Rojas and Dr. Amin), `address`, `telephone`, `geo`, `openingHoursSpecification`, and `priceRange`. Add `FAQPage` schema to the top 5 procedure pages (BBL, breast augmentation, tummy tuck, rhinoplasty, liposuction).
*Why it matters:* Zero structured data means the practice is invisible for FAQ accordion features, knowledge panel data, and star rating snippets. Miami plastic surgery competitors with schema dominate visual SERP real estate.
*Expected outcome:* Eligibility for FAQ rich results and improved knowledge panel within 4–8 weeks.

**2. Build a full Dr. Atul Amin biography page**
Document Dr. Amin's board certifications, training institutions, surgical specializations, awards, and procedural focus areas with the same rigor applied to Dr. Rojas's profile. If he specializes in specific procedures, name them explicitly. Patients researching a two-surgeon practice expect complete profiles for both providers.
*Why it matters:* An undocumented second surgeon effectively cuts the practice in half from a trust perspective. Any patient who Googles "Dr. Atul Amin" and finds nothing will doubt the practice.
*Expected outcome:* Improved trust conversion for the ~40–50% of patients who research both surgeons before deciding.

**3. Establish a RealSelf profile with 20+ structured before/after cases**
Create verified provider profiles for both surgeons. Upload a minimum of 20 before/after case sets for each high-demand procedure (BBL, breast augmentation, tummy tuck), with captions including technique, patient goals, and outcome notes. Respond to Q&A within 48 hours.
*Why it matters:* RealSelf is the highest-intent plastic surgery research platform in the US. No visible RealSelf presence means the practice is missing patients at the exact moment of decision — when they are comparing surgeons and ready to book.
*Expected outcome:* Consultation inquiries from mid-funnel patients within 60–90 days of launch.

**4. Separate the bariatric and aesthetic patient journeys with dedicated landing pages**
Create two distinct site sections: one for surgical/non-surgical aesthetics, one for bariatric/weight-loss services. Connect them with a narrative bridge around body transformation continuity — but keep initial patient journeys separate so that a rhinoplasty prospect doesn't encounter intragastric balloon content on the same page.
*Why it matters:* Patients making $10,000+ surgical decisions actively seek evidence of specialization. A mixed-service menu signals generalist. Two clear journeys signal expert.
*Expected outcome:* Higher per-page conversion rates; improved SEO targeting for distinct keyword clusters.

**5. Build a medical tourism landing page targeting the airport proximity advantage**
Page targets: "plastic surgery near Miami airport," "fly-in plastic surgery Miami," and Spanish-language equivalents. Include: travel logistics, nearby recovery accommodation recommendations, bilingual staff confirmation, post-op follow-up protocol for out-of-state/international patients, and USD financing. Partner with 2–3 nearby recovery suites for mutual referral.
*Why it matters:* Miami's Latin American inbound medical tourism segment is significant and growing. The 5-minute MIA proximity is a material operational advantage most local competitors cannot replicate.
*Expected outcome:* New patient segment with above-average procedure values and zero direct competitor on this specific positioning.

## Long-Term Initiatives (This Quarter)

**1. Build the Bariatric-to-Plastics Pipeline as a Named Program**
Create a formal internal program — working title: *"The Transformation Continuum"* — connecting the bariatric and plastic surgery service lines: (a) dedicated landing page explaining the program arc, (b) sequential consultation flow triggered at patient weight-loss milestones, (c) email automation deploying plastic surgery education at the clinically appropriate 12–18 month post-bariatric window, (d) financing packaging designed around the full transformation arc.
*Business case:* Bariatric patients who achieve significant weight loss are the highest-probability converters for body contouring — abdominoplasty, arm lifts, thigh lifts, breast lifts. They have demonstrated financial willingness to invest and are internally motivated. Acquisition cost is zero — they are already inside the ecosystem.
*Resource requirements:* Email automation platform (Klaviyo or HubSpot), 4–6 content pieces, CRM tagging for bariatric patients.
*Projected ROI:* 10–20 additional body contouring consultations/month from the existing patient base at near-zero acquisition cost.

**2. Resolve the Dr. Rojas Personal Brand Split**
Choose and execute one of two paths: **(A)** 301 redirect `dryoelrojas.com` to Dr. Rojas's bio page on the practice site, consolidating all domain authority and patient traffic under one roof. **(B)** Formalize `dryoelrojas.com` as a professional/media entity (speaking, academic, media appearances) with explicit language linking clinical practice to Provocative Plastic Surgery. The current ambiguity splits SEO authority and implicitly signals that the surgeon's identity is separable from the practice.
*Business case:* Dr. Rojas's personal brand carries real search equity from Newsweek and NYT recognition. Consolidated into the practice domain, it compounds the practice's authority. Split across two domains, it dilutes both.
*Resource requirements:* DNS redirect or web copywriting; 1–2 days of development.

**3. Launch a Patient Intent Content Engine via the Blog**
Conduct structured keyword research targeting Miami plastic surgery + bariatric + medical tourism long-tail queries. Assign 3–5 anchor content pieces to each major service line: "body contouring after gastric sleeve Miami," "BBL recovery week by week," "how to finance a mommy makeover," "ABPS certified plastic surgeon Miami," "plastic surgery near MIA airport." Each post links to the corresponding procedure and gallery page, terminates in a procedure-specific consultation CTA, and includes a lead magnet email capture (e.g., *"The Miami Plastic Surgery Planning Guide"*). Target: 2 posts/month minimum.
*Business case:* The blog infrastructure exists and generates zero value in its current state. Content velocity is the primary organic growth lever available on the Wix platform.
*Projected ROI:* 500–2,000 additional monthly organic visitors within 6–12 months; 3–8 additional consultation requests/month from content traffic.

**4. Evaluate Platform Migration from Wix**
Commission a technical assessment of migrating to managed WordPress (WPEngine/Kinsta) or Webflow. Wix imposes a hard ceiling on Core Web Vitals performance, A/B testing capability, structured data control, and advanced CRO tooling. In a Miami market where $5,000–$15,000/month SEO investment is standard among multi-surgeon competitors, operating on a platform with a built-in technical ceiling is a compounding strategic disadvantage.
*Resource requirements:* Technical SEO audit ($1,500–$3,000), migration development ($8,000–$20,000), 3–4 month timeline.
*Projected ROI:* 20–40% improvement in organic search performance post-migration; unlocks full structured data, A/B testing, and advanced analytics capability.

## Detailed Analysis by Category

### Content & Messaging Analysis
**Score: 62/100**

The practice name "Provocative" is one of the few genuinely differentiated identities in a Miami plastic surgery market dominated by clinical and geography-based branding. It signals confidence, a results-forward aesthetic, and aligns directly with Miami's cultural identity — bold, aspirational, unapologetic. This is a legitimate category-differentiator.

The credential stack supporting the brand is strong and verifiable: ABPS board certification, Newsweek Best Plastic Surgeons three consecutive years, NYT Super Doctors Rising Star (2020/21), Summa Cum Laude academic credentials, ACS/ASPS/AMA memberships. These are third-party validated signals that most boutique practices cannot replicate.

The known tagline — "Miami's premier destination for plastic surgery & med-spa treatments" — is category-generic to the point of invisibility. Every meaningful competitor claims some version of "premier." The tagline immediately squanders the attention earned by the brand name by retreating into undifferentiated language.

The service portfolio is strategically broad and covers the full Miami patient lifecycle: body contouring (BBL, lipo, tummy tuck, arm lift, thigh lift), facial procedures (rhinoplasty, facelift, brow lift, eyelid, neck lift), med-spa (Botox, lip fillers, facial balancing), mommy makeover, and — unusually — bariatric/weight-loss surgery. This breadth creates a lifecycle play that few competitors can credibly offer.

Social proof depth is shallow relative to the brand's claimed positioning. 28 Yelp photos with minimal review count is insufficient for a practice marketing itself as Miami's premier destination, particularly for BBL and breast augmentation patients who are among the most photo-research-intensive demographics in elective surgery. Dr. Amin's credentials are virtually absent from the public narrative — a significant gap for a two-surgeon practice.

### Conversion Optimization Analysis
**Score: 41/100**

Above-average financing infrastructure (PatientFi + Cherry as two separate, indexable pages), procedure-specific gallery segmentation, and a thank-you page signal basic funnel awareness. Airport proximity is a genuine conversion asset for medical tourism patients.

The critical deficit is the complete absence of pricing transparency. Price is the most commonly Googled secondary query after any plastic surgery procedure name. Patients who cannot find even a directional price range exit — and most will not call to ask. This silence loses high-intent prospects before any human contact.

The Wix platform imposes a structural CRO ceiling. JavaScript-heavy rendering produces measurable mobile page speed degradation — Google/Deloitte benchmarks document a 20–30% conversion rate reduction for every 2–3 second additional mobile load time. In a market driven heavily by Instagram and Google Ads mobile traffic, this is a compounding revenue drag.

Four orphaned pages (`blank`, `blank-1`, `blank-2`, `home-2-1-1-1`) are actively indexed. These break funnel flow and signal poor CMS governance to search engines. The consultation flow shows no signs of scheduling automation, response SLA commitments, or intake personalization — table stakes in 2026 for a multi-surgeon practice.

The site appears to rely exclusively on visual gallery content for social proof, missing the separate psychological lever of patient experience narratives (reviews, testimonials). These answer a different patient question: not "do they get good results?" but "what was it like to be a patient here?" — which is what closes hesitant prospects.

### SEO & Discoverability Analysis

Seven segmented gallery pages (BBL, tummy tuck, mommy makeover, face, breast, liposuction, general) create procedure-specific hubs. Crawl rate limiting for low-value bots is configured. A blog infrastructure exists.

However, the practice's SEO posture is structurally weak across the dimensions that matter most in competitive local search.

**Zero structured data** is the most critical gap. No `MedicalBusiness`, `Physician`, `LocalBusiness`, `FAQPage`, or `Review` schema is implemented. Miami plastic surgery competitors with deployed schema capture FAQ accordion features, knowledge panel data, and star rating snippets that directly lift click-through rates. Without schema, the site competes for the same SERP space with a fraction of the visual footprint.

The `*?lightbox=` robots.txt disallow blocks ALL bots from crawling gallery image URLs. Combined with the absence of an image sitemap, gallery images — a high-intent, low-competition discovery channel — are completely invisible to Google Images.

The four orphaned draft pages contaminate the sitemap at an 8.3% rate. Core Web Vitals risk is high: Wix's architecture routinely produces LCP scores above 4 seconds and CLS issues from lazy-loaded gallery assets. At 35 characters, the domain name suppresses direct navigation traffic and branded search transmission.

### Growth & Strategy Analysis
**Score: 52/100**

Three high-value growth levers are structurally available and currently underexploited:

**Medical tourism**: Airport proximity, Miami's global aesthetic surgery reputation, and likely Spanish-speaking staff capacity create a ready-made channel for Latin American medical tourism — a growing segment with above-average procedure values and minimal local competitor response.

**Bariatric-to-plastics pipeline**: The combination of weight-loss and body contouring services under one roof is a self-contained internal referral loop with near-zero acquisition cost. Bariatric patients are among the highest-probability converters for body contouring after reaching target weight — and they are already inside the ecosystem.

**Dr. Rojas personal brand equity**: Award recognitions in named publications create searchable, indexable authority. If the practice is not capturing search traffic for "Dr. Rojas Miami plastic surgery" or "Newsweek best plastic surgeon Miami," that authority is leaking to a parallel domain.

The central growth gap is systemic: channels exist in isolation. Instagram generates content. The blog is inactive as a patient intent engine. Bariatric patients are not programmatically connected to the plastics pipeline. No email nurture system captures the 30–180 day consultation decision cycle. Without systematic nurture, the practice loses the majority of cold and mid-funnel leads to attrition — not to competitors, just to friction.
